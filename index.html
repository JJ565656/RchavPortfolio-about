ME, MYSELF AND I

Hi, I’m Jeane Johan, designer, marketer, and professional over-thinker. Born and raised in Indonesia, I’m currently navigating life as a student at the University of Waterloo, studying Global Business and Digital Arts (GBDA). I specialize in storytelling design, blending digital design, videography, and editing to create visuals that tell a compelling story.

Inspiration is everywhere. For me, it can come from a random thought on a walk, a song that gets stuck in my head, or even a current trend. I take those moments, add my twist, and create something that feels both unique and universally relatable. I hold myself to a high standard, always pushing to make work that’s not only meaningful to me but resonates with others.

And between deadlines? You’ll find me with a playlist that fuels my creativity (because let’s be real, nothing gets done without it.)

CREATIVE PROCESS OR JUST PURE CHAOS?

So, my creative process? It depends. If inspiration hits, I’m golden. I already know exactly where it’s going. If not, I start with a mess of sketches, random ideas, or scrolling through designs until something sparks the idea.

Inspiration comes from anywhere—a weird turn of phrase, a song I can’t get out of my head, even a random trend. If it clicks, it clicks. If not, I keep poking at it until it does. When I hit a block, I take a walk. It’s cliché, but it works. If that fails, I ask my friends to roast my ideas. That always helps.

My tools of choice? Adobe Suite. But I’m always up for learning something new. I like to stay flexible and pick up whatever gets the job done. And if all else fails, I send it to a friend, act like I want feedback, rename the file “final_FINAL_v2,” and pretend I have done my job.

KAY, SO…

At the core of everything I create is storytelling. Design isn’t just about making things look good, it’s about making them feel right. The best work doesn’t just sit there; it pulls you in, makes you think, or maybe even convinces you of something before you realize it. That’s the kind of design I love.

I don’t chase a specific type of project, just the ones that push me to learn, adapt, and do something unexpected.

Rules? Great for structure, not so much for limits. I like to treat them as guidelines, borrowing from what has worked before while twisting it into something new. If I’m not learning, I’m probably overanalyzing, and if I am learning, I’m still overanalyzing, but in a way that makes things better.

The goal is always the same:
keep evolving, keep refining, and never settle for just okay
when better is still on the table.
